Books We Have Read

The Maze Runner by James Dashner
A thrilling dystopian novel where a group of teens wake up in a mysterious maze with no memory of how they got there. They must work together to escape before time runs out.

Howl's Moving Castle by Diana Wynne Jones
A magical adventure about Sophie, a young woman cursed to become an old lady by a wicked witch. She seeks refuge in the moving castle of the enigmatic wizard Howl.

Mrs Dalloway by Virginia Woolf
This novel follows Clarissa Dalloway as she prepares for a party while reflecting on her life choices and her relationships in post-World War I London.

Giovanni's Room by James Baldwin
A powerful exploration of identity and sexuality, this novel tells the story of an American expatriate in Paris who is grappling with his sexuality and relationships.

A Man Called Ove by Fredrik Backman
This heartwarming story centers on Ove, a curmudgeonly man whose life is transformed by the arrival of new neighbors and the unexpected friendships that follow.

Orlando by Virginia Woolf
A unique narrative that spans centuries, telling the story of Orlando, a character who changes gender and experiences various historical periods and cultural shifts.

The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo by Stieg Larsson
A gripping thriller involving a journalist and a hacker who team up to investigate a decades-old disappearance that unveils dark family secrets and corruption.

The Master and Margarita by Mikhail Bulgakov
A fantastical tale blending the story of the Devil visiting Soviet Moscow with a retelling of the trial of Jesus, exploring themes of love, power, and literary freedom.

The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy by Douglas Adams
A hilarious science fiction adventure that follows Arthur Dent, an ordinary human who is whisked off Earth just before it's destroyed to make way for an intergalactic highway.

The Catcher in the Rye by J.D. Salinger
A classic novel about teenage angst and alienation, focusing on Holden Caulfield, a disenchanted youth who narrates his experiences in New York City after being expelled from prep school.

The Road by Cormac McCarthy
A harrowing tale of survival in a post-apocalyptic world, focusing on a father and son traveling through a desolate landscape in search of safety.

The Great Gatsby by F. Scott Fitzgerald
A classic exploration of the American Dream through the life of Jay Gatsby and his pursuit of wealth and the love of Daisy Buchanan in the Roaring Twenties.

1984 by George Orwell
A dystopian novel set in a totalitarian society under constant surveillance, where individuality is persecuted and the regime maintains control through manipulation and fear.
